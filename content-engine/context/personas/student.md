# The Student

## Who they are
College and graduate students balancing lectures, research, papers, and part-time work. They need to capture and produce large volumes of text efficiently, often under time pressure.

## Pain points
- Taking notes during fast-paced lectures means missing key points while typing
- Writing papers and essays is slow and mentally draining, especially for non-native English speakers
- Accessibility needs (learning disabilities, motor impairments) make traditional typing difficult or exhausting
- Free dictation tools are inaccurate or cloud-based, raising concerns about academic work being stored on third-party servers

## How they'd use EnviousWispr
- Dictate lecture notes in real-time during class, capturing ideas faster than typing allows
- Draft essay paragraphs and thesis sections by voice, using LLM post-processing to clean up academic prose
- Record and transcribe study group discussions or office hours for review later
- Use Standard preset for study guide notes, Formal for essay paragraphs — switch styles depending on the assignment

## Tone shift
Approachable and encouraging. Acknowledge the grind without being patronizing. Emphasize that it's free and requires no account — students are allergic to subscriptions and sign-up walls. Highlight speed and ease of setup. Relate to their actual workflow (papers, notes, study sessions) rather than abstract productivity gains.
