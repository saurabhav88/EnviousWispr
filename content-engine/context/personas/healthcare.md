# The Healthcare Professional

## Who they are
Doctors, nurses, and allied health workers who need to dictate clinical notes, referral letters, and discharge summaries throughout their shifts. They work in environments with unreliable WiFi (hospitals, clinics, field settings) and handle extremely sensitive patient data subject to HIPAA and patient privacy regulations.

## Pain points
- Typing clinical notes between patients is slow and error-prone, eating into already-limited time
- Hospital and clinic WiFi is unreliable — cloud dictation tools fail mid-note when connectivity drops
- Cloud-based dictation sends audio to external servers, creating compliance and privacy risk for patient data (PHI)
- Enterprise dictation software (Dragon Medical, Otter) requires per-seat licensing and internet — expensive and impractical at scale
- Switching between EHR windows and a separate dictation app adds friction to an already chaotic workflow

## How they'd use EnviousWispr
- Dictate patient notes, referral letters, and discharge summaries directly into their EHR or notes app between patients
- Use push-to-talk for quick notes during short gaps between patients, and hands-free mode for longer charting sessions
- Dictate prescription notes and care instructions without worrying about connectivity or data leaving the device
- Use the Formal writing style preset for clinical documentation that matches the tone expected in medical records
- Work offline in field settings, rural clinics, or hospital dead zones where WiFi is unavailable

## Tone shift
Lead with privacy and offline capability — these are not nice-to-haves, they are requirements. Acknowledge that EnviousWispr is not a medical device and does not claim HIPAA certification, but emphasize that because audio never leaves the device, there is no cloud transmission of PHI — which removes the biggest compliance concern. Avoid marketing fluff; healthcare professionals respond to direct, evidence-based language. Quantify time savings in clinical terms (e.g., "dictate a discharge summary in 60 seconds instead of typing for five minutes between patients"). Position against cloud dictation tools (Dragon Medical, Otter) on privacy, offline reliability, and cost (free and open source vs. per-seat licensing).
