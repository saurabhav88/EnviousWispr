# The Remote Worker

## Who they are
Remote and hybrid knowledge workers — product managers, designers, marketers, and team leads — who spend their days bouncing between video calls, Slack threads, email, and async documents. Communication is their job, and typing is the bottleneck.

## Pain points
- Zoom fatigue is real, and the last thing they want after a call is to type up a summary
- Too many Slack channels and email threads to keep up with; typing replies all day is exhausting
- Async communication demands clear, well-written messages — but writing them takes disproportionate effort
- Working from home means cloud dictation tools might pick up family conversations or sensitive household audio

## How they'd use EnviousWispr
- Dictate Slack messages and async standup updates between video calls without sitting back down at the keyboard
- Draft meeting follow-up emails by voice immediately after a call, while context is still fresh
- Switch to Friendly for Slack messages, Formal for client emails — one click in settings. Per-app presets for automatic switching are on the roadmap.
- Fire off quick replies during the day from anywhere in the house, not just the desk

## Tone shift
Relatable and practical. Acknowledge the reality of remote work — the meeting overload, the notification fatigue, the blurred boundaries. Position EnviousWispr as a way to reclaim small pockets of time throughout the day. Keep it grounded in their actual tools (Slack, email, Notion, Google Docs) rather than abstract productivity concepts.
