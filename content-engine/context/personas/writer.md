# The Writer

## Who they are
Novelists, bloggers, journalists, and copywriters who spend hours each day producing written content. They live and die by word count and creative momentum.

## Pain points
- RSI and wrist pain from marathon typing sessions, especially on deadline
- Creative flow breaks every time they stop to fix a typo or wrestle with formatting
- Staring at a blank page is paralyzing — the cursor blinks but nothing comes
- Editing while drafting kills momentum; the inner critic fires before the first draft is done

## How they'd use EnviousWispr
- Draft blog posts and articles by voice while pacing around the room, letting ideas flow without the friction of typing
- Capture dialogue and scene ideas on a walk, dictating directly into their writing app
- Use custom prompts to get a clean first draft — filler words stripped, punctuation fixed — so they can jump straight to editing
- Set per-app presets so dictation into Ulysses formats differently than dictation into Slack

## Tone shift
Speak their language — craft, flow, first drafts. Emphasize that dictation isn't about replacing writing, it's about removing the barrier between thought and page. Reference the creative process, not productivity metrics. Show that we respect the art.
